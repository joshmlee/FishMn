About this site & how to read the data

About

This site was built by a lifelong Minnesota angler who wanted an easier way to find great fishing lakes using real data. All survey data comes directly from the Minnesota DNR LakeFinder program. My hope is that this tool helps fellow anglers discover new waters and deepen their appreciation for Minnesota's incredible fisheries.

Understanding the data

CPUE (Catch Per Unit Effort) is the number of fish caught per net per night during a Standard gill net survey. It's the most reliable way to compare fish abundance across lakes — a higher CPUE means more fish were caught relative to the effort put in.

Standard gill nets are the DNR's primary tool for surveying open-water species like walleye, northern pike, and perch. Results are consistent and comparable across lakes and years, making them ideal for ranking.

The trend arrow (↑ green / ↓ red) compares the most recent survey to the previous one for the metric you're ranking by. It gives a quick read on whether a lake's fish population is heading in the right direction.

Survey date filter lets you focus on recent data only — useful for finding lakes with up-to-date information rather than surveys from decades ago.
